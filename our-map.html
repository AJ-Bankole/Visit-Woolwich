Woolwich Town Hall

Woolwich Ferry

London's easternmost crossing point of the River Thames

Royal Arsenal - Dial Arch Square

Home to the Dial Arch, a pub built inside an 18th century armoury

Royal Artillery Museum

Home to many fascinating pieces of history

Royal Arsenal Heritage Site

A green area, decorated with antique arnaments

Thames Path

Woolwich's section of the tranquil pathway which continues for miles alongside the River Thames

Woolwich Arsenal Pier

Heading into Central London? Do it in style by catching a Thames Clipper from Woolwich Arsenal Pier

Thames Barrier

Technological icon which keeps Woolwich (and much more of London) from being flooded by the Thames

Barrack Field

A large park, part of the Royal Artillery Barracks. This park played host to the shooting events during London's 2012 Olympic Games

Shooter's Hill

Notorious for being a highwayman hotspot during the 18th century

Woolwich Dockyard

This area, while modernised, still contains a lot of architechture from centuries past when it was a major industrial hub

Gallions Reach Park

This peaceful park has a large hill from which you can see a fantastic view of Woolwich

North Woolwich

The area on the other side of the ferry. You can also travel between the two sides via foot tunnel

Royal Victoria Park

A small park which is often overlooked- perfect place to enjoy a nice, quiet day

Woolwich Common

Adjacent to Barrack Field, this common is occasionally used to host fairs

Welcome to Woolwich

A district in South-east London, situated on the River Thames. Woolwich is home to a number of historical sites, intertwined with large-scale modernization to create unique area with a rich history & and potential for an even richer future.

History

Dig under Riverside Park, near the Royal Arsenal, and you would unearth the remains of a Roman fort, and traces of an Iron Age settlement. The Anglo Saxon place name 'Woolwich' alludes to a trading settlement or port used for wool, which may have come from sheep raised on the marshes of Plumstead.

Royal Institutions

In 1512, Henry VIII chose Woolwich as the site where his flagship Henri Grace a Dieu would be built, and hence a royal dockyard was established. A rope-making facility soon followed, as did a royal laboratory where explosives, fuses, shot and the like were produced. This was to become the Royal Arsenal at Woolwich, the largest munitions producer in the country. These institutions transformed Woolwich into a prosperous town, which by the mid 19th century was spilling over into the neighbouring parishes of Plumstead and Charlton. Woolwich Common also became the home of the Royal Artillery.

Woolwich Ferry

Much earlier, Woolwich also crossed the river. In 1086, for example, we know that it included a chunk of land on the north side of the Thames, now known as North Woolwich. This remained until 1965, when North Woolwich was transferred to the new Borough of Newham. The free Woolwich Ferry started operating in 1308.

Royal Arsenal

Woolwich's Royal Arsenal was a significant millitary stronghold used by the british armed forces. It was originally used as a Tudor warren, but was purchased by the Office of Ordinance in the 1660's due to it's strategically advantageous location. The area was used to manufacture arnaments which helped Great Britain become the world's major superpower over the next few centuries. During this time the Royal Arsenal grew massively, being right at the forefront of Britain's industrial revolution. By the time of WWI, the Arsenal covered over 1000 acres & employed around 80,000 people
